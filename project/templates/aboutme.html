About Me and Why I got into Coding


My name is Adam Tucker and I am an aspiring Python coder. In last November 2019 I realised that I needed a change from what I have been doing for the past 8 years and set about finding a new direction. I thought back through my career and realised that some of the things I have been most proud and enjoyed the most were creating sense of large data sets to affect change in my category or creating timesaving macros and formulas which turned long monotonous tasks into quick efficient tasks.
I started researching into where I could do this sort of work and quickly realised that but learning to code it would open huge numbers of doors where I could do the work I have enjoyed doing over the past years.


I then looked into which part of coding I wanted to get into and Backend Software and Data Science jumped out at me as being the best fit as it would allow me to target parts of work I had identified as a career.
I seriously considered joining a coding Bootcamp but after some more research I found that all the information is available for free or at a minimal cost online and that as long as I stayed dedicated and committed to learning I would be able to learn in my own time and with no out lay. So, I settled on becoming self-taught.



I choose Python as the stack I wanted to focus on first. I choice Python for a couple of reasons firstly Python is known as a reasonably good first language and when I looked at the syntax and noticed that many were the same as ones I had learned in Excel. Secondly Python had grown to become the most widely used based on a report from Stack Overflow. I have tried as much as possible to only focus on learning Python and libraries which I felt would build on my skills as much as possible.


I have pulled together this website to show off the skills I have learnt during my journey so far. I have built this website using Django and have used Bootstrap to help with the design. I have added a section on Data Science and Machine Learning. In this section I will use Numpy and Pandas explore the data and use the data visualization tools in MatPlotLib and Seaborn to display it.
Finally I will use Cufflinks and Plotly to display market information on a map of the world.
